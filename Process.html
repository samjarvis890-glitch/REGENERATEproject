Complete Transparency

The
Protocol.

A documented chronicle of every stitch, solvent, and structural stabilization performed within our atelier floors.

01

Diagnosis & Chemistry.

Before physical tools engage the hide, biochemical analysis determines the exact pH level of the rot. We perform swab tests to dictate which specific enzymatic neutralizers will stabilize the fibers without causing immediate shock or shrinkage to the vintage material.

Start Assessment
02

Surgical Dismantling.

Rather than patching over, we unpick absolute original seams. Master tailors document every thread curve via macro-photography, ensuring that when the panels are reassembled, the structural tension remains exactly as it was intended in its era of origin.

03

Matrix Fatliquoring.

Applying thick polish is a modern deception. We use raw biological unrefined oils—neat's-foot and lanolin—painted deeply into the dermal layers over a 14-day absorption window. This restores true suppleness from the inside out, totally preventing future cracking.

Uncompromising Tooling.

We utilize historic implements for historic garments.

Brass Riveting

Original hardware matched to era-specific weight and oxidation profiles.

Aniline Dyes

Pure, breathable pigment reconstruction without surface-suffocating acrylics.

Saddle Stitching

Lock-stitches using heavy waxed linen threads ensuring lifetime panel bonds.

Patience is a prerequisite.

True restoration cannot be rushed. Hide fibers require deliberate time to stretch, absorb, and restructure. The average atelier occupancy for a full heritage commission is strictly between 8 to 12 weeks.

Expedited Servicing

We do not offer rush jobs. Interventions involving biological materials follow strict laws of physics and curing times. Rushing results in irreversible structural damage.

Aftercare Guarantee

Every restored garment is returned with a bespoke cedar block and our proprietary atelier conditioning wax. We ensure lifetime support for any structural faults.
