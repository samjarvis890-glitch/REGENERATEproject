The Resurrection of Matrix Oils.

When this piece arrived at the atelier, its condition was categorized as terminal. The original fatliquor—the natural biological oils keeping the hide supple—had completely evaporated over sixty years, leaving a brittle, cracking shell that shed dust with every movement.

Standard repair shops would have painted acrylic filler over the cracks, a temporary and ultimately destructive measure. Instead, we performed an extensive molecular rehydration. We sourced era-correct donor hides for the irreversible damages along the collar stand.

Finally, all original stitching was manually unpicked. We retraced the exact factory needle-holes using heavily waxed linen thread, ensuring the tension distributed perfectly across the new hide structure. The result is structurally sound for another half-century.
